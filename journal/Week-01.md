\## System Planning



Major architectural decisions have been completed.



Current system architecture includes:



\- STM32 NUCLEO-F446RE as the primary controller

\- Differential drive mobile platform

\- Two encoder DC motors

\- Modular subsystem architecture

\- Future Raspberry Pi integration

\- Future MQTT communication

\- Future Home Server connectivity

\- Future self-balancing architecture

\## Hardware Planning



The initial hardware selection has been completed.



Selected hardware:



\### Controller



\- STM32 NUCLEO-F446RE



\### Motion System



\- 2 × 12V Encoder DC Motors

\- 2 × BTS7960 Motor Drivers



\### Sensors



\- MPU6050 IMU

\- 2 × VL53L0X Time-of-Flight Sensors



\### Power System



\- 3S Li-ion BMS

\- 3S 18650 Battery Holder

\- XT30 Connector Set

\- LM2596 Adjustable Buck Converter

\- MT3608 Adjustable Boost Converter

\- Fuse Holder

\- 5A Automotive Blade Fuses



Some components are currently awaiting delivery.



\---



\# Engineering Decisions



Important engineering decisions made during this week:



\- Validate every subsystem before designing custom PCBs.

\- Use modular hardware during early development.

\- Prioritize reliability over unnecessary features.

\- Keep every project phase independently testable.

\- Separate planning from implementation.



\---



\# Lessons Learned



The planning stage required significantly more effort than initially expected.



However, creating a clear engineering architecture before purchasing hardware is expected to reduce future redesign effort.



Finding suitable encoder motors from local suppliers proved to be one of the most challenging aspects of the hardware selection process.
